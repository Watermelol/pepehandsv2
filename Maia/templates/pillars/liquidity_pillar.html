Liquidity refers to the ease with which an asset, or security, can be converted into ready cash without affecting its market price
Every asset has their own places on the liquidity spectrum.
Cash is universally considered the most liquid asset because it can most quickly and easily be converted into other assets.
Liquidity is important for learning how easily a company can pay off it’s short term liabilities and debts.
